\section{Interval Arithmetic on Collections}
\label{sec:approach}

\td{FORMALISM SECTION. Develop the unifying lattice (the title object). Draft worked
out; transcribe it here.}

The analysis tracks, for each glacial variable $x$, an over-approximation of the set
$\mathrm{Reach}(x)\subseteq D_x$ of values it can hold once the namelist is fixed. We
abstract $\wp(D_x)$ uniformly.

\td{Define the parametric base lattice $B$ with $\gamma_B:B\to\wp(D)$, instantiated as
the interval lattice $\mathsf{Int}$ for numeric $D=\mathbb{Z}$ and the flat lattice
$\mathsf{Flat}(\mathcal T)$ for the finite type universe $D=\mathcal T$.}
\td{Define the bounded disjunctive completion $\mathcal C_k(B)=\{F\subseteq B:|F|\le
k\}\cup\{\top\}$, $\gamma(F)=\bigcup_{b\in F}\gamma_B(b)$. "Set of intervals" $=
\mathcal C_k(\mathsf{Int})$; "set of types" $=\mathcal C_k(\mathsf{Flat}(\mathcal
T))$. Give $\sqcup$ (union + rebound/widen), $\bot=\varnothing$, $\top=D$ (the I/O
barrier).}
\td{Whole-program state $=$ reduced product $\prod^\sharp_x \mathcal C_{k_x}(B_x)$
\cite{reducedproduct}. Define the VALUE$\to$TYPE reduction $\rho$: given the program's
discriminator $\delta_x:\mathbb Z\to\mathcal T_x$ (the \texttt{select type}/factory),
$\rho$ refines $x$'s type-set to $\{\delta_x(v):v\in S\}$ from the tag's value-set $S$
--- a singleton tag yields full devirtualization. This is the formal content of C2.}
\td{Define residualization $\mathcal R$ mapping the fixpoint to specializations: the
four optimizations are the four cases keyed by representation (singleton/finite-set/
interval/type-set) $\times$ use (value/index-bound/dispatch-tag). Cross-product for
finite sets; bound-propagation (no enumeration) for intervals.}
\td{Soundness: Galois connection per $B$; disjunctive completion and reduced product
preserve it; monotone transfer $\Rightarrow$ fixpoint over-approximates
$\mathrm{Reach}$. Residual guards partition the config space $\Rightarrow$ exact,
unguarded hot path. Address position vs.\ MEMORY~\cite{memory_cgo24} and frame
honestly as an instance of reduced product (pre-empt the "just reduced product"
rebuttal).}
